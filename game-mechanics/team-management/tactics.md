# Tactics

The greatest managers often happen to be the best tacticians.\
MFL club owners must learn and experiment to find the optimal way to utilize their players, but each contest may warrant a different recipe.&#x20;

## Tactical Adjustments

Managers have an array of options and adjustments to make in the Tactics screen, and will have to decide on the following ahead of each match.

* **Starting Eleven and Substitutes** _<mark style="color:yellow;">\[Substitutes In Development]</mark>_\
  Choose from the players registered for the competition and enter them in the match sheet.&#x20;
* **Formation**\
  Select the formation your ten outfield players will adopt during the match from a list of options.
* **Captain Selection**\
  Assign a captain to your team using the “C” button on the Tactics screen. Once selected, the captain provides stat boosts to the entire starting XI, with the bonuses depending on the captain’s position (e.g. midfielders give +2 PAS and +1 DRI).
* **Team instructions**\
  Choose the main playing style your team adopts when in and out of possession. This may include options related to build-up play, preferred attacking zones, the team's defending style, etc.\
  [Learn more about Team Instructions](https://blog.playmfl.com/changing-the-tactical-game-introducing-team-instructions-573908ad8425)
* **Player Instructions**\
  Give your players individual instructions that affect their tendencies and behavior during matches.\
  [Learn more about Player Instructions](https://blog.playmfl.com/taking-tactics-to-the-next-level-introducing-player-instructions-8fa0935ded17)
* **Team Mentality&#x20;**_<mark style="color:yellow;">**\[Under Consideration]**</mark>_\
  Pick your team's overall approach from various options, which affect the density in different zones of the pitch. Selecting a mentality geared toward attacking, for instance, will encourage more players to make runs, join the attack, or stay forward during set pieces.

## Match Plans

**Match Plans** let managers set tactical changes that trigger automatically during matches.\
They react to scorelines or time windows – for example: _“If losing after 60’, switch Attacking Tendency to More Offensive.”_

You can adjust team instructions, player roles, and formations based on these conditions.\
This adds realism and strategic depth, letting clubs adapt dynamically without live input.

In short: **prepare once, react automatically.**
